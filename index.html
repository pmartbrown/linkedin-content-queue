Pending Approval Credibility
I used to hedge a $26 billion mortgage servicing rights portfolio. I ran P&L for billion-dollar operations at one of the world's largest insurers. So when people ask why I left corporate America to build a private lending operation, here's my honest answer: I saw how the institutional world really works. The best risk-adjusted returns aren't always in the places retail investors are looking. The wealthiest institutions have entire teams dedicated to finding short-duration, secured lending opportunities that most people never see. I wanted to bring that same discipline, institutional risk management, proper documentation, escrow protection, to a market that desperately needed it: creative real estate transactions. Over the last 2+ years, we've deployed: ▶ Roughly $15,000,000 across 100+ deals ▶ 25+ states ▶ Capital deployed, returned, and redeployed Not theory. Not a pitch deck promise. Actual capital at work. The corporate playbook taught me how to manage risk at scale. Now I apply those principles every single day. What's the biggest career pivot you've made, and what did your previous career teach you that still serves you today? #CareerPivot #PrivateLending #RiskManagement #RealEstateInvesting #InstitutionalFinance
