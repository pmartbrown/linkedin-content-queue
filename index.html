Ready for ReviewEducation
"Isn't this just hard money lending?" I get this question a lot. The answer matters more than you'd think. Hard money lending: Capital is lent against a property for 6-18 months. You're exposed to borrower default, property value decline, foreclosure timelines, and market shifts. Transactional lending: Capital goes into escrow for a specific closing. It comes back in 1-30 days. There's no long-term property exposure. Different duration. Different risk profile. Different model entirely. The confusion exists because both live under "private lending." But the risk characteristics aren't remotely the same. If you're evaluating private lending opportunities, duration should be your first question. What's the first thing you look at when evaluating a lending opportunity? #PrivateLending #HardMoney #TransactionalLending #RiskManagement #RealEstateFinance

Ready for ReviewMarket Pivot
Some private credit funds are seeing quarterly redemption requests that far exceed what they can actually honor. Investors want out. The fund gates withdrawals at a fraction of what's been requested. Capital stays locked. This is what happens when funds hold long-duration, illiquid assets and too many investors move for the exit at the same time. The positions can't be unwound fast enough to meet demand. "Private credit" covers a massive range of strategies. Some lock capital for years with limited exit options and gated redemptions. Others cycle capital through short-duration transactions in days. Before committing to any alternative strategy, understand the duration. Understand the liquidity terms. Know exactly what happens when you want your money back. What's the first question you ask about liquidity before committing capital? #PrivateCredit #Liquidity #AlternativeInvestments #RiskManagement #DurationRisk
