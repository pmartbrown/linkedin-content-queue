Hard Money vs Transactional Lending

FactorHard MoneyTransactional
Duration6-18 months1-30 days
CollateralProperty valueEscrow account
Market RiskExposed to declinesZero correlation
Default RiskBorrower dependentTransaction-based
ExitForeclosure processCapital returns at close
Capital StatusLocked in assetFlows through deals

KEY TAKEAWAY

Same industry. Completely different risk profiles. Duration is the differentiator.

"Isn't this just hard money lending?" I get this question a lot. The answer matters more than you'd think. Hard money lending: Capital is lent against a property for 6-18 months. You're exposed to borrower default, property value decline, foreclosure timelines, and market shifts. Transactional lending: Capital goes into escrow for a specific closing. It comes back in 1-30 days. There's no long-term property exposure. Different duration. Different risk profile. Different model entirely. The confusion exists because both live under "private lending." But the risk characteristics aren't remotely the same. If you're evaluating private lending opportunities, duration should be your first question. When Investors Want Out

The liquidity question every alternative investor should ask

LONG-DURATION FUNDS

Capital locked for years

Illiquid underlying assets

Gated redemptions

"We'll get back to you."
Redemption requests exceed what the fund can honor

SHORT-DURATION STRATEGIES

Capital cycles in days

Transaction-based returns

No lock-up mismatch

Capital flows through deals
Duration matches the strategy

THE QUESTION

Before you invest: what happens when you want your money back?

Some private credit funds are seeing quarterly redemption requests that far exceed what they can actually honor. Investors want out. The fund gates withdrawals at a fraction of what's been requested. Capital stays locked. This is what happens when funds hold long-duration, illiquid assets and too many investors move for the exit at the same time. The positions can't be unwound fast enough to meet demand. "Private credit" covers a massive range of strategies. Some lock capital for years with limited exit options and gated redemptions. Others cycle capital through short-duration transactions in days. Before committing to any alternative strategy, understand the duration. Understand the liquidity terms. Know exactly what happens when you want your money back. What's the first question you ask about liquidity before committing capital? #PrivateCredit #Liquidity #AlternativeInvestments #RiskManagement #DurationRisk
