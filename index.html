Hard Money vs Transactional Lending

FactorHard MoneyTransactional
Duration6-18 months1-30 days
CollateralProperty valueEscrow account
Market RiskExposed to declinesZero correlation
Default RiskBorrower dependentTransaction-based
ExitForeclosure processCapital returns at close

KEY TAKEAWAY

Same industry. Completely different risk profiles. Duration is the differentiator.

"Isn't this just hard money lending?" I get this question a lot. The answer matters more than you'd think. Hard money lending: Capital is lent against a property for 6-18 months. You're exposed to borrower default, property value decline, foreclosure timelines, and market shifts. Transactional lending: Capital goes into escrow for a specific closing. It comes back in 1-30 days. There's no long-term property exposure. Different duration. Different risk profile. Different model entirely. The confusion exists because both live under "private lending." But the risk characteristics aren't remotely the same. If you're evaluating private lending opportunities, duration should be your first question. What's the first thing you look at when evaluating a lending opportunity? #PrivateLending #HardMoney #TransactionalLending #RiskManagement #RealEstateFinance

THE REAL QUESTION

How much of your portfolio is truly uncorrelated to public markets?

Most portfolios aren't as diversified as people think. You own stocks across sectors. Maybe some bonds. An index fund or two. Perhaps a REIT. But when the market drops 10%, everything moves together. That's correlation. And it's the enemy of real diversification. True diversification means owning assets that don't move with public markets at all. Assets where the return driver is completely disconnected from what the S&P does on any given day. That's what draws sophisticated investors to certain alternative strategies. Not chasing higher returns. Removing the correlation that makes a "diversified" portfolio drop in unison. When you look at your portfolio, how much of it is truly uncorrelated? #Diversification #AlternativeInvestments #PortfolioStrategy #RiskManagement #InvestorMindset
